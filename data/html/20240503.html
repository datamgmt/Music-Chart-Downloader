        Chart Review 03/05/24

        Radio 1 should stop hijacking the chart with their Big Weekend garbage.

        Anyways, what's happening lads! I'm here, I'm probably not queer, but that never stopped anyone else from posting stuff on here, did it? Right then, the chart. Bank Holiday Monday is part of my 5-day weekend (my college allows me to skive off Tuesdays for some reason, but I'm not complaining), and day 1 will be dedicated to dissecting what's hot and what's not in the charts right now. Because there's nothing more fun than looking at how awful some songs are and yet still do well. You know full well who I'm directing this abuse at, America. Then again, Perrie's disastrous showing despite copious radio airplay is definite proof of how eclectic we are.

        And speaking of slightly pretty females...

        The album chart looks as interesting as it always does, with Taylor Swift casually cropping up a second week at the top with The Tortued Poets Department, an album that hasn't had the best critical reception compared to other Swifty efforts this decade. Not that anyone will be complaining or anything, but given dad-pop and formerly important 80s duo the Pet Shop Boys are at 2 with their new effort, it does feel a little cheap. Come on, give them it. They haven't had a No.1 album since 1993, just let them have this moment for once. PLEASE. I know it's been 15 years since they last had an actual hit but it wouldn't hurt to give them a chance, would it? St. Vincent's at 5, and Jess Glynne's first album since 2018 is in the Top 10 as well. Not bad, considering the singles off it have performed so badly that I'm surprised her career didn't end on the spot. Good for JESS, that's all I'm gonna say.

        As for the singles, err.... Would you wanna be Sabrina Carpenter right now?

        I mean you would, obviously, given the success she's having right now, but before 2024, the most notable things about her career thus far was denting the back half of the 40 a couple times, getting into a tiny spat with Olivia Rodrigo, and having to live with the guilt knowing that your aunt is Bart Simpson. That was until Feather went BANANAS on TikTok and gave her some much-needed radio excess, and since Espresso dropped three weeks ago, she's just been going from strength to strength. The soon-to-be 25 year old from Quakertown, Pennsylvania now has her first ever chart-topper on these shores, as Espresso nudges up 4 spots thanks to Spotify and their gang of streaming hoodies. And best of all, it doesn't make me wanna contemplate why songs like Nonsense and Skin never did much. Good on ya Sabrina, but just be aware, if you get overplayed in the upcoming months, don't blame me. I'm just a dimwit on a chart website posting utter drivel.

        TayTay can at least take some credit from these unexpected circumstances, as she still has 3 song in the Top 10 today. Fortnight only drops to 2, Down Bad slides down to the foot of the best-sellers ladder, and The Tortured Poets Department has been replaced in the general public's affections by I Can Do It With a Broken Heart at 8, which is probably better, or probably not. I don't know, really, it's all in one ear and out the other with this album in truth. On a more positive note, it turns out Lil Nas X fever is gonna strike all over again as Shaboozey divebombs up to 6 with A Bar Song (Tipsy), in a move that DEFINITELY won't anger the racist folk that think country and pop shouldn't ever collide. Eh. Maybe the Cowboy Carter thingy took all the comments out of them.

        Naturally, the rest of the chart is as dead as it always is, apart from the climbers of Lay Banks, Chappell Roan, David Guetta and Badger of course, not matter how catastrophic some of them are. Joining them in the 'is this worth it?' category at 31 is the only other new hit today, from Tommy Richman, who has been hanging out with Brent Faiyaz recently to get his name out there, and has now completely proven why he's such a, quote, 'exciting prospect'. MILLION DOLLAR BABY has been doing bits on the streaming sites thanks in no small part to two separate versions going viral. The normal one, and a so-called VHS version that's exactly the flippin' same. Go figure. So you know, good for him, it's his first Top 40 single, but the track is very mid and I've forgotten everything about it since the chart show ended. Then again, according to old Tommy on his Spotify bio, "EVERYTHING IS PUNK!", so clearly I'm not cut out to dissect such mediocre rap records. Or something.

        Meanwhile, the songs at 38 and 39 are somewhat more plausible, given they're directly linked to that new Amy Winehouse biopic that's been doing the rounds recently and spearheading critics with how good it is. Now, personally, I don't really think her music was all that rad or anything, but I'm in the minority apparently, as the Mark Ronson bandwagon-jumping Zutons-massacring Valerie re-enters at 38 for it's first Top 40 jaunt in SIXTEEN YEARS, with Back To Black clawing it's way here for the first time since kicking the bucket in 2011. It's great and all that she's back to give the hit parade a right bop, but it wouldn't hurt for us to nick a time machine and get her to lower those alcohol levels. Maybe then the charts right now would sound a lot better. And maybe I wouldn't make such snide comments about equally successful artists.

        Outside the 40, and Kendrick Lamar hit back at Drake on Wednesday with Euphoria, his first new music since 2022 and as long as an average trip to the shops. It's only on 50 after two days of streaming, so, you know, expect it to do better next time. Elsewhere and Disturbed's dance remake of The Sound of Silence genuinely makes me want to commit genocide on the people who made it, Tems is back at a measly 58, Kygo's latest effort flops at 75, Aitch embarrasses everyone at 78, and all the other re-entries are the most soul-crushing songs you'll see all year. No one wanted Lewis Capaldi back in the chart again, alright?

        Next week's albums come from the likes of Sia, Frank Turner, Kelly Jones from the Stereophonics and Rachel Chinouriri, while the singles sees the returns of K-Trap, Becky Hill, Barry Can't Swim, Blossoms, and Meghan Trainor. So clearly next week is pretty much DEAD. Ah well.

        See you next time then.
