        My thoughts & observations on the UK charts as always…

        ‘Take Me Back To London’ by Ed Sheeran ft Stormzy spends its fifth consecutive week at #1 on the singles chart! It received 53,000 combined chart sales for the week, which is 14,000 ahead of its nearest competitor.

        ‘Higher Love’ by Kygo & Whitney Houston was the most played song on national radio for a fourth non-consecutive week!

        ‘Take Me Back To London’ also continues its YouTube reign, as it’s been #1 there for four consecutive weeks now! It loses 11.6% of its weekly views to 2.84 million. There were also three debuts inside the Top 10; ‘Don’t Call Me Angel’ (#2, 2.44 million), ‘Nookie’ (#4, 1.68 million) & ‘Wiley Flow’ (#9, 1.34 million).

        LIZZO - GOOD AS (#23)
        Woah… I did not expect this! Yeah, I knew ‘Good As ’ would be breaking into the Top 40 this week, but I didn’t think it’d be anywhere near as high enough to give Lizzo now officially her highest charting hit to date! That said, honestly, this may even be able to top ‘Juice’ for me & I’ve been singing praises for that song ever since I reviewed it. I could just say that this is another older Lizzo track that promotes self-confidence & being happy with who you are, but I feel that Lizzo’s incredible pipes & effortless charm elevates this to a much better level, even if the backing vocals are a little flatter than they probably should be. In terms of production, while I think that the drum mix was a bit overdone, the horns & piano melody are what your attention is immediately drawn to! So yeah, we might get a certain commenter bringing up this artist every day for the next few weeks, but at least the song truly impressed me. Therefore, strong 8/10!

        TIESTO & MABEL - GOD IS A DANCER (#37)
        All you need to do is just look at the names here to know that this’ll be a massive hit in bars & clubs across Britain, as well as having potential to be big in the charts, but all of that is essentially confirmed when you actually hear the song itself! I love how it so clearly resembles a lot of Tiesto’s early roots with the 90s-house feel, yet it’s also something that could’ve fit just as well on ‘High Expectations’ that came out earlier this year, since Mabel’s voice continues to remain distinctive & convincing! I think what really caught my interest was how bubbly the Caribbean inspired melody felt, but on the other hand, Mabel’s vocals are clearly auto-tuned way more than they need to be here (in fact, her completely natural tone might’ve been an even better fit) & I’ve never liked the religious comparisons in dance songs, especially when you focus on Mabel’s choreography in the official video, but hey, this’ll still easily bring brightness & fun to your weekend. So for that, solid 8/10!

        LOUD LUXURY & BRYCE VINE - I’M NOT ALRIGHT (#16 CANADA) [WORLD HIT]
        With two new arrivals, I think it’s fair to say this new singles chart didn’t interest anyone all that much! So, on that note, I’ve looked into what’s big abroad which could potentially make it’s mark over here in the near future! The more intriguing feature of this song is that while the horns & synths in the drop might give this a considerable groove, the content is rather bittersweet, which sees a reflection on a relationship that is falling apart because of Bryce thinking the woman he’s with is out of his league, so he turns to drinking & smoking to get over it.
