        My thoughts & observations on the UK charts as always…

        - ‘Promises’ by Calvin Harris & Sam Smith is at the top of the singles chart for a fifth straight week! It has a lead of around 12,000 combined sales over its nearest competitor, ‘Happier’ by Marshmello & Bastille.

        - On top of that, it’s also six consecutive weeks at #1 on the airplay chart for ‘Promises’!

        - ‘I Love It’ by Kanye West & Lil Pump was the most watched song on YouTube in the UK again this past week. It was viewed 2.76 million times, down 28.5 percent from last week.

        - Meanwhile, Rita Ora makes chart history this week as ‘Let You Love Me’ becomes her 13th Top 10 hit, which is now more Top 10s than any other British female artist!

        - I’m also especially surprised but pleased that ‘High Hopes’ is now Panic! At The Disco’s highest charting song in the UK now & I really hope for it to go Top 10 next week.

        - LADY GAGA & BRADLEY COOPER – SHALLOW (#13)
        I’m really impressed by how much this managed to sell this week & it makes me think that ‘A Star Is Born’ could go to #1 on the albums chart next week if the sales remain consistent throughout the week. Anyway, this was a spectacular choice for a lead single! ‘Shallow’ is a slow-building conversation over a beautiful picked acoustic guitar melody, which burst into a body of drums & pedal steel guitar. The vocals are amazing too, Bradley Cooper's deep & soulful delivery might not be among the best I’ve heard in recent films, but he still has a lot of earnestness here to let Lady Gaga blow everyone’s mind with her part; starting off sturdy before gradually building into a heavy wail with massive belting. Either way, if this song is anything to go by, ‘A Star Is Born’ is going to be an interesting tale indeed! As a rating, I’m thinking a strong 8/10 at the moment.

        - LIL WAYNE FT KENDRICK LAMAR – MONA LISA (#21)
        I have to admit that I underestimated the hype that there was for this album, but with tracks like this, it’s definitely understandable! The first feature that stands out is that the production is incredibly sinister, opening with wild piano notes, a dark beat & even some violins later in the song, which fits the also sinister content well, as a woman sets up a plot to rob her “boyfriend”. The level of description here is certainly good & while Lil Wayne’s part is decent, the instrumentation drops out around three & a half minutes into the song for Kendrick’s super quick delivery, among some of the best I’ve heard from him in a while now! Lil Wayne’s emotion at the very end of this hits hard too, especially the intense anger after his almost tearful outro. Honestly, it stands out so much from all the grime & trap we have in the charts today, so hopefully there’s more of this to come. Strong 7/10 for me!

        - LIL WAYNE FT XXXTENTACION – DON’T CRY (#28)
        So ‘Tha Carter V’ was a better album that I was expecting, but this is still the worst track from me & a real struggle to get through! Firstly, if you’re telling somebody not to cry, then sampling XXXTENTACION’s horribly droning painful wail was a terrible idea. I understand that Lil Wayne is trying to overcome the situations he’s faced throughout his life, but he sounds a lot less engaged & focussed than other tracks on this album. The verses just didn’t have the impact that Lil Wayne was trying to go for sadly, but the hook was intolerable for me. Sorry, I’m not a fan, 3/10.

        - DJ SNAKE FT SELENA GOMEZ, CARDI B & OZUNA – TAKI TAKI (#30)
        I’m still mostly undecided on the production from DJ Snake, which has an unusually dark sound for this reggaetón vibe that we’re used to. The synth loop and drum programming are definitely infectious, but the former is pitched way too high that it’s a little annoying unfortunately, which is a problem considering that’s the main underlying beat for the song. Ozuna’s first verse might be a bit disappointing due to him having very little charisma, but Cardi B brings slightly better bars due to a consistently solid flow, however Selena Gomez’s verse is arguably the best, with her bringing a very flirtatious approach in both English & Spanish! I might not be won over completely yet, but I’m liking more with each listen, so for now, light 6/10!

        - MK X JONAS BLUE X BECKY HILL – BACK & FORTH (#37)
        It took me a few listens to get into this & while I don’t see it being a huge hit in the same way that ‘17’ was, it could very well come close! What surprised me is exactly how upbeat & dynamic this was; yeah, I like MK’s warm piano-house style, as well as Becky Hill showing off her soulful vocals with a lot of range, even if I think she could’ve done a better job on the hook, it falls a little flat for me. Then you have Jonas Blue for the bridge, where the rhythm speeds up & there’s a Becky Hill vocal loop over a heavier beat, which due to the huge amount of groove in that means that I think it’s the best part of the song! I guess for a song about the highs & lows of a relationship, the lyrics are a bit too blunt to make it really stand out, but it’s still well executed for an October dance track. On that note, light 7/10!

        - On the albums chart, ‘Blood Red Roses’ becomes Rod Steward’s ninth #1. It accumulated around 41,000 combined sales since it was released last Friday.

        - The rest of the Top 5 is: ‘Dancing Queen’ by Cher (debuts at #2), ‘The Greatest Showman’ soundtrack (2-3), ‘Kamikaze’ by Eminem (1-4) & ‘Tha Carter V’ by Lil Wayne (debuts at #5).

        - Looking ahead to next week, these songs could be entering the Top 40 for the first time: ‘Funky Friday’ by Dave & Fredo, ‘Without Me’ by Halsey, ‘I Want You To Freak’ by Rak-Su, ‘Polaroid’ by Jonas Blue, Liam Payne & Lennon Stella, ‘1999’ by Charli XCX & Troye Sivan, ‘Stoopid’ by 6ix9ine ft Bobby Shmurda, 'One Shot' by Mabel, a couple of tracks from the soundtrack to ‘A Star Is Born’ & maybe even more.

        - Thanks for reading everyone! I hope you all have a good weekend. :)

The dark horse here could be a completely unexpected debut straight in at #1 though - Dave and Fredo's 'Funky Friday' has shot straight to #1 on the Spotify chart, and #2 on the Apple Music chart (where the #1 is 'Eastside', which is now on ACR). If it can stay there for the rest of the week on both platforms, it could go straight to #1. I do think it's more likely that we'll just see a Top 10 debut, though, which would still be great. I'm not really sure why this track's doing so well since Dave's previous best was #17 with 'No Words', and his last track 'Hangman' debuted and peaked at #30, but I really like Dave as an artist so it'd be great to see him get his first Top 10 hit.

        As for the new entries this week, I wasn't really a fan of most of them. 'Shallow' would be a light 5/10 for me, I like Kendrick's part in 'Mona Lisa' but I'm not a huge fan of the rest of the song, so probably a strong 6/10, 'Don't Cry' is definitely the weakest track that I've heard from 'Tha Carter V' (though I've only heard 4 tracks) with a weak 4/10, I'm very similar to you on 'Taki Taki'; at the moment I'd give it a 6/10 but it is growing on me with every listen, and 'Back & Forth' is the best out of the new entries in my opinion, with a 7/10.

        I'm surprised that 'Kamikaze' fell all the way to #4 this week, and I think now that it's behind 'The Greatest Showman' it won't be going back to #1. If it had fallen to #3 behind the 2 new albums i could see it going back next week or the week after, but if it's behind 'The Greatest Showman' it probably won't get ahead of it again. I'm also kind of sad that 'YSIV' could only manage to debut at #19, and that it couldn't beat the #13 debut of 'Bobby Tarantino II'. I really want Logic to get bigger in the UK; he's one of the best rappers around at the moment.

        'Funky Friday' is basically guaranteed to enter next week, and 'Without Me' seems pretty likely as well. A few tracks from 'A Star is Born' look likely as well.
