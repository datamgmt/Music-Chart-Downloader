        My thoughts & observations on the UK charts as always…

        ‘Senorita’ by Shawn Mendes & Camila Cabello reigns supreme on the singles chart for a sixth week in total! Now Camila’s longest UK #1 hit, it accumulated 53.000 combined sales, including 6.15 million streams, over the past seven days.

        ‘Higher Love’ by Kygo & Whitney Houston was the best selling song in the country for a second consecutive week, while also becoming the new airplay #1!

        ‘Old Town Road [Remix]’ by Lil Nas X ft Billy Ray Cyrus unbelievably still dominates YouTube, with an 18th week in a row at #1 there! It loses 8.1% of its views, falling to 2.99 million for the week.

        After five months at #1 in the US, ‘Old Town Road’ finally loses the #1 spot on the Billboard Hot 100 (to ‘Bad Guy’ by Billie Eilish).

        JORJA SMITH FT BURNA BOY - BE HONEST (#18)
        Woah… the BRITs weren’t wrong giving Jorja the Critic’s Choice Award a couple of years back. She got the highest debut of the week in a chart of reasonably strong competition among the five new arrivals! Although, after hearing this collaboration, it’s not too hard to see why either. Jorja Smith still has a very enticing delivery despite wanting to get straight to the point about a guy who fancies her & while she remains the main feature of the song, I do like Burna Boy’s easily recognisable Nigerian accent in his part as well & you can tell that the two have collaborated before, despite perhaps needing a bit more chemistry between them. While the groove might be notably tight, it’s also surprisingly bumpy, which is thanks to Izy & Cadenza’s input after also working with Sam Smith in the past. In general, I’m not sure I dig this as much as most of the tracks on that last album, but Jorja Smith is still certainly worth keeping an eye on! It’s a strong 6/10 for this one.

        HEADIE ONE - BOTH (#22)
        I had no idea that Headie One even had a mixtape out this week & while it didn’t do too much on the albums side of things, it still gave us this as the big breakout solo single from him! What stands out initially after hearing this is that while he insists he’s the “king of drill”, the credentials in this song aren’t what typically defines the genre. For one, he samples ‘Free’ by Ultra Nate from 1998 (which is actually a dance anthem!), but even outside of that, the track doesn’t hit with the dark & ominous beat that you’d expect, but more a fast-paced skittering one instead. Headie One himself rides that beat rather well, expect in the pre-chorus where he goes so muffled that I can’t even understand what he’s saying that well. Overall, I can appreciate that he’s putting in more effort than some of his peers, but I can’t say that I’ll remember it beyond this week. Very light 6/10!

        TAYLOR SWIFT - LOVER (#23)
        I’m fairly sure this will hold up well next week thanks to Taylor’s album of the same time being released today, but I was more focussed on whether she would be able to continue improving with every pre-released single she put out, so was she able to do it? See, I nearly gave a perfect rating to ‘The Archer’, but I think hearing the song tells me why I didn’t. The fact that this is a country ballad already makes it feel so nostalgic to me, but it’s also the level of passion & devotion that Taylor sings with (probably to Joe Alwyn) which is where it really hits the heart for me. As you would expect, it’s a rather slow tempo song, which allows the boom of the snares & the main acoustic guitar line to truly stand out. No joke, this could be a late contender to become my favourite song of the entirely of 2019, because it’s a beautiful song & proof that the “old Taylor” didn’t die after all, she came back to slay! As for ‘Lover’ itself, it joins ‘SOS’ & ‘Senorita’ in an elite achievement… getting a 10/10!

        NORMANI - MOTIVATION (#30)
        So it turns out Fifth Harmony now has a second artist who can chart consistently over here! After collaborations with Khalid & Sam Smith, Normani now finally has some solo attention over here. With this, it’s certainly got huge potential, that’s for sure! The general vibe of this reminds me of the early 90s smashes where pop & R&B first started to fuse, but the best element of all of this has to be the trumpets that shine in the bridge! Although, it’s Normani herself who really brings this song together, where she sings with confidence & finesse, especially the “Take a look at me now.” line! I don’t agree with the Beyonce comparisons that people are already starting to make, but yeah, I’m going to be rooting for this to have more success! For now, light 8/10.

        SAM FELDT FT RANI - POST MALONE (#34)
        I’ve been wrongly predicting this’ll make the Top 40 for the past few weeks, but I’m pleased I finally got it right this time around, haha! It might be a bit late to dominate the summer airwaves, but with the jolly synth & colourful groove, it’s still infectious nonetheless. The lytics might just be about partying like most dance smashes these days, but they’re relatively innocent given RANI’s smoother verses & resisting from outright belting in the hook. I’m not 100% on board with that wailing drop, but the rest of the song seems to have effortless production around it. I don’t expect this to do anywhere near as well as ‘Show Me Love’ did, but I’m still glad to see Sam Feldt get a second charting hit! For me, this is a solid 7/10.

        On the albums chart, ‘No.6 Collaborations Project’ by Ed Sheeran returns to #1 for a fifth non-consecutive week.

        The rest of the Top 5: ‘Divinely Uninspired To A Hellish Extent’ by Lewis Capaldi (3-2), ‘No Man’s Land’ by Frank Turner (debuts at #3), ‘We Are Not Your Kind’ by Slipknot (1-4) & ‘When We All Fall Asleep, Where Do We Go?’ by Billie Eilish (stationary at #5).

        Looking ahead to next week, these songs could enter the Top 40 for the first time: ‘What Am I’ by Why Don’t We, ‘We Got That Cool’ by Yves V ft Afrojack & Icona Pop, 'F**k, I'm Lonely' by Lauv & Anne-Marie, 'My One' by Wiley ft Tory Lanez, Kranium & Dappy, 'Dance Monkey' by Tones & I, another Taylor Swift track (probably ‘The Man’, ‘Cruel Summer’ or ‘I Forgot That You Existed’) & maybe even more.

        Thanks for reading everyone! I hope you all have a good weekend. :)
