        Brief review of today's chart:

        'Own It' is yet again not the real #1 of the week - which is unfortunate, as it's a great song, and I really hoped it would pull ahead of 'Dance Monkey' to be the true #1 of the week, but alas, it did not. In any case, 'The Box' will likely be overtaking it soon, which is hardly something to be thrilled about either - I really don't see the appeal of the song, and the 'eeh-ur' ad-lib is grating.

        The new debuts this week are mostly fine - Selena's 'Rare' was a bit underwhelming, I definitely prefer her previous two singles to it. 'Life Is Good' really seems like a poor mash-up of two completely different songs - Future's part is very generic and forgettable trap, but Drake's bit was actually nice, I kind of wish it could have been drawn out to a full-length standalone song. Stormzy's diss track was probably the best debut of the week personally, although the beef seems to be mostly over, so I don't see the track sticking around, plus I'd rather have 'Audacity' in its place. And finally, Halsey's track was actually a pleasant surprise to hear, although none of her singles from the album have really disappointed.

        The top 40 section of the chart should actually feel a bit more real, since there aren't 20 tracks with arbitrarily cut-in-half streams, but it doesn't, mostly because 'Dance Monkey' is the actual most-consumed track of the week, and there's at least three more songs that should be top 40 without ACR ('hot girl bummer', 'Good as ' and possibly 'Higher Love'). Which, in turn, actually makes the chart look a bit worse. Why are 'Old Town Road' and 'bad guy', tracks from May, able to still be top 40, but 'hot girl bummer', a song that first charted in late October, deemed 'too old'? Why did 'Memories' get a manual reset back to SCR, but not 'Dance Monkey', for example? Sigh... I feel like it's pointless to continue asking this at this point.

        My thoughts & observations on the UK charts as always

        Own It by Stormzy ft Ed Sheeran & Burna Boy rules the singles chart for a third consecutive week.

        Dont Start Now by Dua Lipa is still the biggest song on British radio!

        Dance Monkey by Tones & I leads the national YouTube chart for a 13th non-consecutive week! It even increases 8.5% in views from last week to 3.16 million for the past seven days. The big debuts for this tracking week include Disappointed by Stormzy at #2, Savage by MIST at #3 & (unfortunately) Yummy by Justin Bieber at #5!

        Im incredibly pleased to announce that Blinding Lights becomes the most downloaded song over the past week! I might disagree with the charts being based on just sales, but at least that chart has an amazing smash at #1 this week.

        FUTURE FT DRAKE - LIFE IS GOOD (#3, Worst Of The Week)
        So here we are, officially Futures biggest hit in the UK that was no doubt helped massively by the Drake feature & honest question, outside of Mask Off, do you remember what any of his songs even sound like? I only ask this because I certainly dont & sadly, I could just say the same about this new collaboration too! I know theyve worked together multiples times beforehand, so I dont even mind them having their parts so clearly separated, since it actually causes this to be different than a lot of other trap hits. What I certainly didnt like is how disruptive the transition is, so much that this sounds more like a Drake song mashed up with a Future song, rather than the same hit with both of them on it, just like between Travis & Drake on Sicko Mode! That being said, the former is still saying about how he wont be shut down by haters & the latter is still mumble rapping about luxuries, so nothings really changed & I honestly dont understand why this got so big outside of name recognition. Either way, Ill forget this existed probably by the time I finish typing up this post, so for that, solid 4/10!

        STORMZY - STILL DISAPPOINTED (#21, Best Of The Week)
        Its not even a question as to who won this beef at this point! I hadnt even heard Wileys Eediyat Skengman diss tracks to begin with, but nobody seemed to care for them outside of a small amount of YouTube trending, not even getting that many views. That being said, if you make a threatening comment to assault an incredibly famous rappers mother & claim that Ed killed grime music, yeah, youre not getting my support! Of course, I just love how Stormzy bites back here, where he gets extremely personal with WIleys family members, suggesting that all of the men in his family are weak & he mocks his mothers situation in response. Now, I know that this is incredibly insulting & brutal content, but its a diss track, its supposed to be! However, its Stormzy being the lyrical mastermind that he is, along with his most hard-hitting flow to date & the outright rage he pumps out during the entire track, with sounds phenomenal over the rumbling bass, which is all concluded by 24 hours to reply!. He rants, then he challenges his rival with a blunt & clear message. Stormzy, can you please start putting diss tracks on your studio albums? Heavy Is The Head wouldve done even better if it has this on it. For me, while I dont expect well hear too much of the Wiley-Stormzy drama in the future, this is a solid 8/10 from me!

        SELENA GOMEZ - RARE (#28)
        Despite how well the album did, I thought there would be a bit more of a single impact for Selena Gomez this week! That said, other than Lose You To Love Me getting an incredbly small rebound, the title track did manage to debut. Unfortunately, if the rest of the album is like it, I can see why fans are more disappointed with this album is comparison to, say, Revival! Sure, that dominant guitar groove that runs throughout the song undoubtedly will stick with you, along with Selena Gomez herself being fairly smooth & confident here, but I cant help but feel that her upper range is a lot more strained this time round & the hook falls incredibly flat, along with the fact thats theres no real build-up to it either. Ultimately though, while it seems like an obvious pick for the radio, it doesnt give me the impression that itll go very much further here on the official chart, if at all. I mean, its not bad, but I was hoping this would be better after Lose You To Love Me wowed me so much, although Rare itself gets a light 6/10 from me!

        HALSEY - YOU SHOULD BE SAD (#37)
        You know, going into Manic which got released today, I have absolutely no idea what artistic direction Halsey wants to head in going forward! Yes, shes primarlily a pop artist, but shes had touches of R&B, EDM & even rock in some of her recent singles, so you could imagine my confusion when I found out this was country-inspired! That being said, I dont mean that in a bad way, because I found myself listening to this a lot over the past two days. Yes, Halseys vocals are still a bit too raw or raspy for my liking personally & thats always been an issue Ive had with her singing anyway, but despite that, she can really convey both her anger & her relief of this break-up effectively, with a somewhat catchy melody attached to it too! Even the twangy elements of the production impressed me here too, except at the end of each hook where it just sounds messy & failing to carry any bombast with it. So yeah, it might not be her best single, especially when Eastside & especially Closer are just in a different ballpark & I still enjoy Nightmare more among singles as a lead artist, but this has me very intrigued to hear more of what her new album will bring! For me, Im thinking a solid 7/10. Thanks @coolocelot101 for letting me know about this!
