        My thoughts & observations on the UK charts as always…

        - ‘Perfect’ by Ed Sheeran spends a sixth week in a row atop the singles chart! With ‘River’ by Eminem ft Ed Sheeran just behind at #2, Ed breaks another Official Charts record; he is now the artist with the most weeks at #1 & #2 simultaneously, with a total of 14!

        - ‘Perfect’ also remains at #1 on the airplay chart for a seventh consecutive week.

        - The highest debuting song of the week is ‘Finesse [Remix]’ by Bruno Mars ft Cardi B at #7, giving Bruno his 11th Top 10 appearance & Cardi B her first! I was very pleased that this was announced as the next single from ‘24k Magic’, because the original was one of my favourite tracks on that album in my opinion. That’s because Bruno pays tribute to 90s R&B by bringing back New Jack Swing & making this sound like such a fun throwback! With more listens, believe it or not, Cardi B’s verse actually makes me enjoy this even more than the original; her flow is getting better with every song she appears on! Some of the lyrics don’t really make a lot of sense at times, but that’s only a very minor issue when both artists are bringing so much attitude to the song! I loved it from the first time I heard it & I really hope this sticks around to become a massive hit.

        - Liam Payne & Rita Ora collaborate on ‘For You’, which is featured on the upcoming ‘Fifty Shades Freed’ soundtrack & it enters the chart at #11 this week! Once again, the first single from the soundtrack is a team-up between a former 1D member & a huge female pop star, which I can see why they’d do given what the ‘Fifty Shades’ trilogy is about. However, it’s mostly Rita as to why I like this; she opens the song by introducing to the blossoming relationship, but she admits to being in a better state of mind when Liam is by her side. Her vocals are sincere & smooth, then evokes warm thoughts as she remains transfixed on her lover throughout the hook. Then we get to Liam & honestly, he plays the role more of a featured artist than a lead artist here & it’s annoying that his voice seems auto-tuned, given that he sounds decent with his natural vocals. Liam & Rita don’t seem to have a huge amount of chemistry for a soundtrack to a film like this, but I will say that I like the harmonies a lot. Overall, it’s not amazing, but there’s still a fair bit of quality here & I’m happy that these two collaborated. I could see myself getting into this more in the coming weeks!

        - Justin Timberlake makes his highly-anticipated comeback by announcing his upcoming fifth studio album & the lead single from it, called ‘Filthy’, debuts on the chart at #15 today! This came as big surprise, considering I’d heard that JT was apparently going down a country-inspired route on his new album, but this is anything but country! If anything, it reminds me of his earlier discography, as it sounds like a 00s dance classic. It starts with that typical moment Timbaland moment of bombast, with those guitar licks that are well over-the-top & the cheerful “Hey”s. What I think caught a lot of people off-guard was how it morphs into a sleek funk banger, along with the blinding lashes of synth & an addictive bassline! What was a little disappointing was that JT’s vocals seem both limited & monotonous, but even with that, he still has a lot of charisma here as usual! The lyrics are either very $exy or dismissing the haters, but he sounds convincing in both of these with his delivery. I will admit that this did take a few listens to grow on me & the general response so far has been rather polarising, but with a Super Bowl performance & an album release at the start of February, I could see this going higher very soon (at least cracking the Top 10), so I’m looking forward to seeing how this does.

        - Continuing with songs for soundtracks, 'All The Stars' by Kendrick Lamar & SZA, from 'Black Panther', debuts at #33 in the new chart. I'll be honest, I've found a lot of pop music taken from superhero films to be either completely generic, or collaborations that didn't stick the landing, or both. That said, 'All The Stars' doesn't fit into anything of those categories, although I did expect better from a team-up between these two acts. There's not a memorable in either the writing or the production. but Kendrick raps well enough about conflict & resolve, while SZA delivers a big & hopeful anthemic hook over the stamping drums & slick streaking synths. Overall, this is certainly listenable, but there's not a lot I'm going to recommend here either.

        - Next up, rising 44-37, ‘Bad’ by Steel Banglez ft Yungen, Mostack, Mr Eazi & Not3s enters the Top 40 on its seventh week charting. I wasn’t really sure what to expect from this, considering it a big collaboration between five upcoming grime artists & I think I like this a little bit more than I should. That’s because I’m not a huge fan of the lyrics, where a guy is only in love with a girl because she does bad things, but the way they deliver those lyrics makes it seem like it’s an awesome thing to be doing some things that are wrong, which it clearly isn’t. Most of the acts here go into their more boring flows, but I have to admit that Mostack’s hook is incredibly addictive & throwing in a bouncy tropical beat works much better than expected here. Overall, I don’t see it having a massive replay factor for me, but it’s at least one of the more tolerable songs you hear on urban radio at the moment.

        - Following up her recent #1 hit, Dua Lipa ascends 55-38 after its debut last week, thanks to the release of an official video for the song. What I find incredible is that a relatively new act is entering the Top 40 with the seventh official single from her self-titled debut album. I’m certainly a fan of this, but not as much as everything else she’s given out before. I did find the drums that are crisp & even nearly militaristic. Her vocals don’t have the range that her other songs do, but she still has that power to her voice that I really like. Lyrically, the song is similar to ‘New Rules’ in the sense that she’s getting over her ex, that there’s no more chances for him & she won’t make the same mistakes & have her heart broken by him again. That said, her delivery is a bit more intense & angrier, which I can appreciate her for, although I understand that because that chorus Is so centred around the F word, it might have trouble getting much radio traction. That said, if this does become a hit, I’ll be completely fine with that. Decent song!

        - Finally, after debuting on last week’s chart, ‘Bartier Cardi’ by Cardi B ft 21 Savage climbs 54-40, giving Cardi her second UK Top 40 appearance as the lead artist. After the disaster that was ‘Bodak Yellow’, I was expecting her to be one of those rappers who I’d much rather hearing on a guest verse rather than their own discography, but ‘Bartier Cardi’ might prove me wrong. Firstly, 21 Savage is probably the best I’ve heard him, even though he still provides some gangster lyrics, but with how Cardi B switches up her flow a bit & how there’s a lot more personality than ‘Bodak Yellow’ here, Cardi B didn’t really need 21 Savage to feature on this in my opinion. Both rappers bring fast & diverse flows to this song & 21 Savage is having a bit more fun in this one, but there’s also fewer punchlines & more repeated rhymes from Cardi B. There’s nothing notable to say about the production other than the lumbering trap beat. Overall, I’m nowhere near as mad at this as I am ‘Bodak Yellow’, but I’m not sure how much I’m going to remember this going forward.

        - On the albums chart, the soundtrack to ‘The Greatest Showman’ rises 4-1! I highly recommend checking it out if you haven’t already.

        - The rest of the Top 5 this week: ‘÷’ by Ed Sheeran (1-2), ‘Too Good At Goodbyes’ by Sam Smith (2-3), ‘Human’ by Rag’n’Bone Man (3-4) & ‘Revival’ by Eminem (stationary at #5).

        - I would expect Camila to debut at the spot next week & while I’ve not heard her debut album yet, I’ve loved all of her solo music so far, so I have every expectation that it’ll be very good!

        - Looking ahead to next week, these songs could enter the Top 40 for the first time: ‘My My My!’ by Troye Sivan, ‘Strangers’ by Sigrid, ‘Let Me Down’ by Jorja Smith ft Stormzy, ‘End Game’ by Taylor Swift ft Ed Sheeran, ‘IDGAF’ by Dua Lipa & Future & maybe even more.

        - I mostly enjoyed the debuts this week & I like seeing ‘I Know You’ hit the Top 40, but it’d be cool to see ‘Breathe’ make it too!

        - Thanks for reading everyone! I hope you all have a good weekend. :)
