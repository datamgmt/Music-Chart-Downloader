        My thoughts & observations on the UK charts as always…

        - ‘Thank U, Next’ by Ariana Grande is #1 on the singles chart for a sixth consecutive week, with a 9,000 combined sales lead. It is now the longest #1 by a female artist since ‘Bleeding Love’ by Leona Lewis in 2007.

        - In only its second week on the chart, ‘Nothing Breaks Like A Heart’ by Mark Ronson ft Miley Cyrus reaches #1 on the national airplay chart.

        - ‘Thank U, Next’ is #1 for a second week on the YouTube chart, with 7.52 million plays!

        - There are 12 Christmas songs inside this week’s Top 40, while this time last year, there were 17.

        - XXXTENTACION – WOAH [MIND IN AWE] (#37)
        Unless any of you can’t tell by now, I was never a fan of XXXTENTACION’s music. At best, his songs were barely passable & at worst, he’s provided us with some of worst album tracks of the entire decade! With this, believe It or not, there was actually a considerable amount of potential here; particularly with that jingly beat & XXXTENTACION’s verse about providing for his family as well as his troubled past & particularly telling his mum that he would make it big one day. Unfortunately, those lyrics only last for eight short lines & the rest of the song is just “Woah” repeated with extended syllables, as well as his continually poor annunciation throughout. This might be one of his better new songs, but when we’re getting a avalanche of Christmas songs re-entering & climbing the Top 40 at the same time, I’d rather listen to those than check out the rest of ‘Skins’. Light 5/10.

        - On the albums chart, ‘The Greatest Showman: Soundtrack’ returns to #1 for the first time since July. Overall, this is its 22nd non-consecutive week at #1, but it has best combined sales week yet shifting 57,000 copies since last Friday.

        - The rest of the Top 5 is: ‘Staying At Tamara’s’ by George Ezra (4-2), ‘Love’ by Michael Buble (stationary at #3), ‘Unchained Melody’ by Rob Orbison (debuts at #4) & ‘Odyssey’ by Take That (stationary at #5).

        - Looking ahead to next week, these songs could enter the Top 40 for the first time: ‘We Built This City’ by LadBaby, ‘Light Up’ by Flakefleet Primary, ‘Imagine’ by Ariana Grande & maybe even more.

        - Thanks for reading everyone! I hope you all have a good weekend. :)
